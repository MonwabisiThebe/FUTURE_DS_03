## Marketing Funnel & Conversion Performance Analysis

### Project Overview
This project analyzes user behavior in an eCommerce platform to understand how users progress through the marketing funnel (view → cart → purchase).

The goal is to identify conversion bottlenecks, user behavior patterns, and opportunities to improve revenue performance.

---

### Objective
- Analyze user movement across funnel stages  
- Calculate conversion rates and drop-off points  
- Understand behavioral patterns in customer journeys  
- Generate actionable recommendations to improve conversions  

---

### Tools & Technologies
- Python  
- Pandas  
- Matplotlib  
- Seaborn  
- Jupyter Notebook  

---

### Dataset
- Dataset: eCommerce Behavior Data (Multi-category store)  
- Sample Size: 100,000 records  
- Key Features:
  - Event type (view, cart, purchase)  
  - Product and category information  
  - Pricing data  
  - User session tracking  

---

### Funnel Analysis

#### Funnel Structure
View → Cart → Purchase

#### Key Metrics
- Visitor to Cart Conversion: 3.27%  
- Visitor to Purchase Conversion: 5.87%  
- Overall Drop-off Rate: 93.45%  

---

### Key Insights

- Severe Drop-off at Early Stage  
  Over 93% of users leave without purchasing, with the largest loss occurring after product views.

- Non-Linear Purchase Behavior  
  Purchases exceed cart activity, indicating users can complete transactions without adding items to cart.

- Low Engagement Beyond Viewing  
  Most users browse products but do not take further action, suggesting weak engagement or low purchase intent.

- Price Sensitivity in User Behavior  
  High-priced products attract significant attention but convert less effectively, indicating pricing impacts decision-making.

- Category-Based Conversion Differences  
  Certain product categories convert significantly better than others, reflecting differences in user intent and product demand.

---

### Recommendations

- Improve product page design to increase user engagement  
- Optimize pricing strategies for high-value products  
- Reduce friction in the purchase process  
- Focus marketing on high-converting categories  
- Enhance recommendation systems to guide user journeys  

---

### Conclusion
The primary challenge in the funnel is not attracting users, but converting them. By addressing early-stage drop-off, pricing perception, and category performance, businesses can significantly improve conversion rates and drive revenue growth.
